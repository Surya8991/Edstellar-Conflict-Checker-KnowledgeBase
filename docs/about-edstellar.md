# About Edstellar

Edstellar is a global corporate training and skill-development platform that delivers instructor-led training (ILT), virtual instructor-led training (VILT), and blended learning to enterprise teams worldwide.

## What Edstellar Does

- **Instructor-led corporate training** — onsite and virtual, delivered by a vetted global trainer network.
- **Catalog of 2,000+ courses** spanning technical (cloud, data, cybersecurity, DevOps, AI/ML), behavioral (leadership, communication, DEI), functional (sales, HR, finance, project management), and compliance topics.
- **Managed training services** — sourcing trainers, scheduling batches, handling logistics, tracking attendance, post-training assessment and reporting.
- **Skill management platform (Skillible)** — competency mapping, learner enrollment, batch tracking, feedback collection, analytics dashboards for L&D teams.

## Who It Serves

Enterprise L&D, HR, and people-operations teams at mid-market and large organizations across North America, EMEA, APAC, and India. Typical buyers are L&D managers, learning ops leads, and CHROs procuring training at scale.

## Delivery Modes

| Mode  | Description                                                          |
|-------|----------------------------------------------------------------------|
| ILT   | Classroom / onsite, trainer travels to client location               |
| VILT  | Live virtual sessions over Zoom / Teams / Meet                       |
| Blended | Mix of self-paced content + live sessions                          |
| 1-on-1 Coaching | Executive coaching, leadership development                 |

## Operational Footprint

- Trainer network spans 100+ countries, 50+ languages.
- Sessions run across most major timezones simultaneously, which makes scheduling and conflict checking a non-trivial problem (see [conflict-rules.md](conflict-rules.md)).

## Why a Conflict Checker

At Edstellar's scale — thousands of trainers, tens of thousands of batches per year, multi-timezone delivery — manual schedule validation is error-prone. A single trainer double-booked across two clients in different timezones is a customer-visible failure. The Conflict Checker exists to make that class of error impossible at booking time rather than discoverable at delivery time.
